Spaghetti Carbonara

A classic Italian pasta dish with creamy egg sauce, pancetta, and parmesan cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces (225 grams) spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus extra for serving
  • 4 slices of pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions in a large pot of boiling salted water until al dente. Reserve about 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining the spaghetti. While the spaghetti is cooking, whisk together the eggs and grated Parmesan cheese in a small bowl. Season with a pinch of black pepper. In a large skillet, cook the diced pancetta or bacon over medium heat until crispy. If using garlic, add it to the skillet and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant. Reduce the heat to low. Add the cooked spaghetti to the skillet with the pancetta or bacon, tossing to combine. Remove the skillet from the heat and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot spaghetti. Toss the spaghetti continuously until the eggs thicken and coat the pasta evenly. If the sauce seems too thick, add a little bit of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up. Season the spaghetti carbonara with salt and more black pepper to taste. Serve immediately, garnished with extra grated Parmesan cheese and chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Chicken Stir Fry

A quick and easy Asian-inspired dish with tender chicken, colorful vegetables, and savory sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

In a bowl, mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and hoisin sauce. Add the sliced chicken to the bowl and toss to coat. Let it marinate for about 15 minutes. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry for 4-5 minutes until cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside. In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the sliced onion, bell pepper, broccoli florets, and julienned carrot to the skillet. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and toss everything together to combine. Drizzle the sesame oil over the stir fry and toss once more to evenly distribute. Season with salt and pepper to taste.Serve the chicken stir fry hot over cooked rice or noodles.
